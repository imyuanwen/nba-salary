As a big NBA fan, I always want to do something related. As there is a lot of analysis and reports of players and teams, much fewer visualizations have been done focused on salary. I'd like to know who earned the most in the last couple of years, which team spent the most money on players salary, and other interesting questions I can find from the data. I think many other NBA fans who are interested in NBA stats and data visualizations will love this project.

I found two interesting datasets on data.world. Go to the datasets. Both of the datasets were updated to 2018. The first dataset contains 24 different attributes about players, but I used only part of them in this project, which is presented on the table below.

The visualizations in this project were all made in Tableau. These two datasets were connected by the "player_id."

Visualization 1

This visualization is a scatter plot that describes how much total salary each team paid from 1984 to 2017. Different colors of circles show different teams. The color scheme is chosen from the theme color of each team. You can click the team to highlight the data. The little grey line shows the median value of salary for each season. The chart shows the salaries didn't change much between 2009 to 2013. The reason behind it is the NBA salary cap didn't change in that period. Two of the outliers are marked with labels. The New York Knicks paid a much higher salary in 2005 but ended up with the last place in the Eastern Conference. I tried to add the record of each team for each season to the visualization. I failed to find a dataset that can connect to the present dataset. I plan to add the record in the next version of this project.

Visualization 2

In addition to team salary, I made another visualization about the salary of each player from 1984 to 2017. A box plot is added for each year to show the distribution. You can see that Michael Jordan was really an outliner at the end of the 90s. He earned almost the same much money in 1998 as Stephen Curry earned in 2018. You can filter players by different teams.

Visualization 3

Players with higher draft picks suppose to have a better career and earn more money. I want to explore this effect, so I made a scatter plot about player salaries and draft picks (only include 1st to 30th and undrafted players). Players like Kobe Bryant, Tony Parker, and Brad Miller (Undrafted) made way more money than players with the same draft pick positions. Some undrafted players like Brad Miller and Ben Wallace earned more money than the median value of 1sk pick players.

Visualization 4

Following the last visualization, I wonder how much each undrafted players earned. This visualization is a horizontal bar chart that shows every undrafted players' total salary through 2018. The grey line shows the average total salary of all the undrafted players. When you hover your mouse on each bar, it will show your more stats of the player.

Visualization 5

How many scores a player can drop in a game is the most straightforward index of his performance. Thus, I made a scatter plot that shows the relationship between average career scores and the total salary of each player. Some outstanding players have been marked with labels. When you hover your mouse on each bar, it will show your more stats of the player. (By the way, George Gervin should’ve made more money. 😢 )

Visualization 6

This chart is my favorite one 🤪, which shows the Top 10 Expensive Players In A Single Game. Jonathan Kerner played three seasons and only one game. But he made $716,000 of his career, which made him the most expensive player in a single game. Kobe and Michael are shown below them and colored with brown color for comparison. When you hover your mouse on each bar, it will show your more stats of the player.
